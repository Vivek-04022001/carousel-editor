TITLE: The Psychology Behind Why You're Liked More When You Care Less.
SUBTITLE: (but most people get this backwards)

== body ==
let's be clear about what "caring less" actually means.

It is not coldness. It is not pretending people don't matter.

It means your sense of self isn't hanging on whether this specific person approves of you right now.

== body ==
so where does the "care less" effect actually come from?

When you need a person to like you, they feel it.

The slight over-explaining. The quick agreement. The laugh that comes a beat too early.

== term ==
EYEBROW: BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS
TERM: Outcome dependence
EXAMPLE: e.g. re-reading a text to check if they "still" like you.
Needing a specific reaction from someone before you can feel okay. It quietly hands them the controls.
